Regions

United States

The United States has a rich history of professional wrestling with various promotions such as WWE, AEW, and Impact/TNA Wrestling. Styles range from the brawling brutes to high-flying and technical wrestling. WWE, the largest wrestling promotion, often showcases a mix of storytelling-driven matches and high-energy, athletic contests. This appraoch is often called "sports-entertainment" instead of professional wrestling. In contrast, promotions like AEW emphasize a more athletic and competitive style with a focus on long-form storytelling.

United Kingdom

The UK has produced many talented wrestlers and is known for promotions RevPro Wrestling, Progress Wrestling, and ICW. British wrestling often combines strong technical wrestling with a strong emphasis on storytelling. The UK's brand of wrestling was defined by the promotion World of Sports, with its focused on technical mastery and submission holds. In recent years., British wrestling has undergone a renaissance. Progress Wrestling, known for its grassroots approach, emphasizes strong storytelling with a mix of technical prowess. ICW (Insane Championship Wrestling) brings a more edgy and hardcore style to the scene.

Mexico

Mexican wrestling, known as Lucha Libre, is famous for its high-flying acrobatics and colorful characters. AAA and CMLL, the major Mexican promotions, showcase high-flying acrobatics, lightning-fast sequences, and a unique blend of athleticism and theatrics. While often known as an acrobatic style, old-school Lucha libre featured a more technical style Luchadores (wrestlers) often wear masks, adding an element of mystique. Some of the biggest events are lucha de apuestas matches, translated to bet matches. Luchadors put their hair or masks on the line. Lucha Libre is not just a sport; it's a cultural phenomenon that combines athleticism with storytelling in a way that is unparalleled.

Japan

Japan has a diverse wrestling scene with promotions like NJPW, AJPW, and Stardom. Japanese pro-wrestling (called Puroresu) are characterized by hard-hitting moves, usually to emphasize realism. Wrestlers showcase a wide array of strikes, powerful slams, and intricate submission holds. New Japan Pro Wrestling (NJPW) is the most well-known promotion in Japanese, embodying "Strong Style". All Japan Pro Wrestling (AJPW) has a style called King's Road, which emphasizes longer matches with storytelling told my moves. Stardom, a promotion focused on women's wrestling from Japan (also known as Joshi), combines athleticism with compelling storytelling, showcasing the depth of talent in the Japanese wrestling scene.
